Q: What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

A: How nonuniform I am when writing (even if it should be a little formal), how hard it is for me to focus on introspection, how bad I am at remembering things.


Q: What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Values and empathy, if you’re not working freelance then you don’t want to scare your work-partner off (and want to work well with them), if you’re working freelance you’ll probably want to stay on the good side of any clients.


Q: What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

A: What surprised me most about human skills learning is it’s focus on growth. I was expecting to learn more about how to stay in the right mindset (specifically for not getting stuck on errors) and working with others. (Not to suggest that focusing on growth is a bad thing, just wasn’t expected.)


Q: What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

A: Doing the Human Skills learning ;-;.


Q: Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

A: I feel like this one has been answered for me, “Tech employers aren't looking for top grades or the person with the most outstanding code. They're looking for exceptional human beings who will enhance the culture of their team.”


Q: Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time?

Nope, not at all.


Q: Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

A: Ehhhh no? (I want to say yes because programming practice is more fun, but without these skills it’s not like you can hold a career on practice alone, plus the growth focus helps with practice so it’s not like the two are entirely separated.)
